Section 1

Understanding Tax

⚖️ Direct Tax

  • Levied on income or wealth of a person
  • Burden cannot be shifted to another person
  • Payer and bearer are the same person
  • Example: Income-tax

🔄 Indirect Tax

  • Levied on price of goods or services
  • Burden can be passed on to another person
  • Incidence is shifted to the consumer
  • Examples: GST, Customs Duty
📜
Constitutional Basis — Article 265 "No tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law." Income-tax is governed by Entry 82, Union List (7th Schedule, Article 246) — Parliament has exclusive power to legislate on income tax (excluding agricultural income).

Section 3 · Section 4

Charge of Income-tax

⚡ Section 4 — The Charging Section

This is the backbone of income-tax law. Tax is charged:

  • At rates prescribed by the Annual Finance Act or the Income-tax Act
  • On every person specified under Section 2(31)
  • On total income earned during the Previous Year (not Assessment Year)
  • In accordance with the provisions of the Act
⚠️
Exceptions to PY taxation rule: Sections 172, 174, 174A, 175, and 176 — income of a PY may be assessed in the PY itself (e.g., shipping income of non-residents, persons about to leave India, etc.)

Section 4 · Computation

15 Steps to Compute Total Income & Tax Liability

  1. 01

    Determine Residential Status

    Classify as Resident & Ordinarily Resident (ROR), Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR), or Non-Resident (NR). Also covers Deemed Resident (always RNOR).

  2. 02

    Classify Income Under Five Heads

    Salaries | Income from House Property | PGBP | Capital Gains | Income from Other Sources

  3. 03

    Compute Income Under Each Head

    Apply charging & deeming provisions. Allow permissible exemptions (e.g., agricultural income fully exempt) and head-specific deductions. Note: Sec 14A disallows expenses relating to exempt income.

  4. 04

    Apply Clubbing Provisions

    Income diverted to spouse, minor child, etc. is clubbed back into the income of the person who diverted it to prevent tax avoidance (Chapter 4).

  5. 05

    Set-off / Carry Forward of Losses

    Intra-head set-off first, then inter-head adjustment (with restrictions). Unabsorbed losses can be carried forward (Chapter 5). Default regime: loss from HP cannot be set off against any other head.

  6. 06

    Compute Gross Total Income (GTI)

    Aggregate net income under all heads after clubbing and set-off.

  7. 07

    Deductions from GTI (Chapter VI-A + Sec 10AA)

    Available only under optional tax regime. Includes 80C (LIC, PF), 80D (medical insurance), 80G (donations), 80TTA (savings interest), etc. Only select deductions under default regime.

  8. 08

    Compute Total Income (TI)

    GTI − Deductions = Total Income. Round off to nearest ₹10 (Sec 288A).

  9. 09

    Apply Applicable Tax Rates

    Apply slab rates (default regime u/s 115BAC or optional regime). Special rates apply to LTCG, lottery winnings, specified STCG, etc.

  10. 10

    Surcharge & Rebate u/s 87A

    Surcharge on IT when total income exceeds ₹50 lakhs. Rebate u/s 87A: up to ₹12 lakh TI (default) or ₹5 lakh TI (optional regime) for resident individuals.

  11. 11

    Add Health & Education Cess

    4% on (Income-tax + Surcharge). Applied after the rebate u/s 87A.

  12. 12

    Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT) — Sec 115JC

    Applicable under optional regime to preserve tax base. Tax credit of excess AMT can be claimed in subsequent years. Not applicable under default tax regime.

  13. 13

    Choose Beneficial Tax Regime

    Compute under both regimes. Non-business assessees (individuals/HUF/AOP/BOI) can switch every year. Business assessees have restrictions on switching.

  14. 14

    Advance Tax & TDS / TCS

    Advance tax in 4 instalments: 15 Jun, 15 Sep, 15 Dec, 15 Mar. Presumptive taxation: one instalment by 15 Mar. TDS deducted by payer and remitted to Govt via RBI/SBI/authorised bank.

  15. 15

    Tax Payable / Refundable

    Gross Tax − Advance Tax − TDS/TCS = Self-Assessment Tax or Refund. Round off to nearest ₹10 (Sec 288B). Pay on/before due date of return filing.

Section 14A — Disallowance of Expenses on Exempt Income Any expenditure incurred to earn exempt income is not deductible under any head. The CBDT prescribes the method to determine such expenditure. This applies even when exempt income has not accrued in the relevant previous year.

Section 115BAC

Tax Regimes at a Glance

🟦 Default Tax Regime (Sec 115BAC)

  • Basic exemption limit: ₹4,00,000
  • Rebate u/s 87A for TI up to ₹12,00,000
  • Most exemptions/deductions NOT available (no LTC, no housing loan interest on SOP, no Chapter VI-A except 80CCD(2), 80CCH(2), 80JJAA)
  • Loss from HP cannot be set off
  • AMT u/s 115JC not applicable
  • Non-business assessees can switch every year

🟧 Optional / Normal Tax Regime

  • Basic exemption limit: ₹2,50,000
  • Rebate u/s 87A for TI up to ₹5,00,000
  • All exemptions & deductions available (Chapter VI-A, LTC, HRA, housing loan, etc.)
  • Chapter VI-A deductions in full
  • AMT u/s 115JC applicable
  • Business assessees face restrictions on switching back

Section 2 · Key Definitions

Important Definitions

Section 2(7)

Assessee

Any person by whom tax or other sum is payable. Includes persons under assessment proceedings, deemed assessees, and assessees-in-default. Every assessee is a person, but not every person is an assessee.

Section 2(8)

Assessment

The procedure by which income of an assessee is determined. May be a normal assessment or a reassessment. Dealt with in detail at Final level.

Section 2(31)

Person (7 Categories)

Individual · HUF · Company · Firm · AOP · BOI · Local Authority · Artificial Juridical Person. AOP/BOI are persons even if not formed for income.

Section 2(24)

Income (Inclusive)

An inclusive, not exhaustive, definition. Includes profits & gains, dividends, perquisites, allowances, capital gains, lottery winnings, subsidy/grant from govt, keyman insurance policy receipts, etc.

Section 2(9)

Assessment Year (AY)

The 12-month period following the Previous Year during which income of the PY is assessed to tax. Runs from 1st April to 31st March. Current AY = 2025-26.

Section 3

Previous Year (PY)

The financial year immediately preceding the Assessment Year. Tax is charged on income earned in PY but assessed in AY. For a new business: from date of setting up to 31 March.

Section 2(1A)

Agricultural Income

Rent/revenue from agricultural land in India; income from such land by agriculture or agricultural operations; income from a farm building used in connection with such operations.

Section 2(14)

Capital Asset

Property of any kind held by an assessee (movable, immovable, tangible, intangible). Excludes: stock-in-trade, personal effects, agricultural land in rural areas, etc.

7 Categories of "Person" — Section 2(31)

🧑
Individual

Natural person only. Includes minor, person of unsound mind. Assessed via guardian if minor.

🏠
HUF

Hindu Undivided Family. Jain & Sikh families also assessed as HUF. Daughters are coparceners since 6 Sep 2005.

🏢
Company

Indian company, foreign company, or entity declared as company by CBDT. Domestic vs. Foreign classification.

🤝
Firm

As per Indian Partnership Act, 1932 and LLP Act, 2008. Minor admitted to benefits treated as partner.

👥
AOP

Association of Persons. Any person (incl. company, firm) can be member. Formed for joint enterprise.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑
BOI

Body of Individuals. Only individuals as members. Executors, co-trustees. Common will may or may not exist.

🏛️
Local Authority

Municipal committee, district board, port authority. Exempt for services within own jurisdictional area.

⚖️
Artificial Juridical Person

Residual category. Deities, Bar Councils, Universities. Not a natural person but has juristic personality.

🎯
AOP vs BOI — Key Distinction BOI: only individuals can be members. AOP: any person (including companies/firms) can be a member. In AOP, members voluntarily come together with a common will. In BOI, common will is not necessary.
🏠 HUF — Schools of Hindu Law

📍 Dayabhaga School

  • Prevalent in West Bengal & Assam
  • No right by birth; right to property arises only on death of father
  • Father and brothers are coparceners

📍 Mitakshara School

  • Prevalent in rest of India
  • Right to property acquired by birth
  • Every child born acquires a right/share in family property

Section 2(1A) · Section 10(1)

Agricultural Income

🌾 What Qualifies as Agricultural Income?
  • Rent/revenue derived from agricultural land situated in India
  • Income derived from such land by agriculture or agricultural operations (cultivation, tilling, irrigation, etc.)
  • Income from a farm building used in connection with agricultural operations on such land (must be on/near the land, occupied by cultivator/receiver of rent)
  • Income from nursery operations (seedlings/saplings grown in nurseries)
⚠️
Agricultural Income is FULLY EXEMPT u/s 10(1) — not included in Total Income. However, it IS considered for computing tax rate (partial integration method) when an individual/HUF/AOP/BOI has both agricultural and non-agricultural income exceeding the basic exemption limit.

Previous Year · Assessment Year

PY & AY — Key Concepts

📅 Previous Year (PY)

  • Financial year preceding the AY
  • Income earned in PY is taxed in AY
  • For new business: from date of setting up → 31 March
  • All sources of income share the same PY
  • Current PY = 2024-25

📅 Assessment Year (AY)

  • Year in which income of PY is assessed
  • Runs from 1 April to 31 March
  • Return of income filed in AY
  • Current AY = 2025-26
  • Tax rates of Finance Act of AY apply
🎯
Exceptions — Income of PY Taxed in PY Itself: Section 172 (Shipping income of non-residents) · Section 174 (Persons leaving India) · Section 174A (AOP/BOI/Artificial Juridical Person formed for specific event/purpose) · Section 175 (Persons likely to transfer property to avoid tax) · Section 176 (Discontinued businesses)
